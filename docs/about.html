What this is

Every US trading-day morning before market open, this site refreshes a volatility screen across the NYSE + NASDAQ common-stock universe (~6,500 tickers). It surfaces names with notable recent price movement in two price ranges, using three complementary definitions of "movement".

This is a personal research artifact, open-source, free, no sign-up. The math is published below in full. Use it as a starting point for your own research — nothing here is a recommendation.

The two buckets

Bucket Price range Variation threshold Catches
A — Mid-priced $300 – $800 ≥ $15 weekly Volatile mid-caps
B — Penny $0.79 – $20 ≥ 20% weekly Penny / small-cap momentum

Both buckets also require 30-day average dollar volume ≥ $1,000,000 to filter out illiquid names where a quoted "move" doesn't reflect real trading activity.

Three measures of "weekly variation"

Each computed over the trailing 5 trading sessions (holiday-aware — if Memorial Day falls in the window, "5 sessions" might span 7 calendar days). A ticker qualifies for its bucket if any one of the three exceeds the threshold. The screen shows all three values side-by-side, with badges indicating which triggered.

1 · Range — max(High) − min(Low)

The highest intra-week high minus the lowest intra-week low.

  • Catches: choppy, two-sided volatility. A stock that swung up to $100 and down to $80 within the week, even if it ended the week at $90.
  • Misses: smooth trends. A stock that gained $1 every day for 5 days has a small range (just the daily volatility) but a big total move — that's caught by 5-day return below.
  • Bucket A: ≥ $15 dollar swing.
  • Bucket B: max(H)/min(L) − 1 ≥ 20% — the relative form. A penny stock going $1 ↔ $1.20 has a 20% range.

2 · 5-day return — |Close[t] − Close[t−5]|

The dollar (or percent) distance from where the stock closed 5 sessions ago to where it closes today. Absolute value — direction doesn't matter for the screen.

  • Catches: directional trends, sustained moves. The stock that ground up $5 over the week even though no single day was eventful.
  • Misses: round-trips. A stock that goes $100 → $120 → $100 has zero 5-day return despite huge intra-week movement (the Range measure catches that one).
  • Bucket A: ≥ $15.
  • Bucket B: |Close[t]/Close[t−5] − 1| ≥ 20%.

3 · Max daily move — max(|daily ΔClose|) over the 5 sessions

The single biggest one-day close-to-close change in the window.

  • Catches: event-driven spikes. The stock that was flat 4 days, then moved $20 on day 5 (earnings, M&A, FDA decision, downgrade).
  • Misses: slow grinders. A stock with $3 daily moves and a $15 weekly total has Max daily = $3 — that's caught by 5-day return.
  • Bucket A: ≥ $15 single-day swing.
  • Bucket B: ≥ 20% single-day swing.

Why three measures? Three mover archetypes.

"The stock moved a lot this week" is genuinely ambiguous — there are at least three distinct mover types, and any single measure misses two of them:

Archetype Range 5-day ret Max daily
Choppy mean-reverter high low medium
Momentum trender medium high low
News-event mover low low high

OR-combined inclusion lets all three archetypes onto the list. The side-by-side display lets you sort or filter on whichever you care about. A stock that triggers all three at once (RNG + 5D + MAX badges all amber) is genuinely unusual — both choppy AND trending AND event-driven in the same week.

How to read the index page

  • Two sortable tables: Bucket A on top, Bucket B below. Default sort: largest Range first.
  • Click a column header to re-sort by that column.
  • Type in the column filter (under the column name) to filter live.
  • Trigger column shows three badges per row:
    • RNG — the Range measure triggered
    • 5D — the 5-day return measure triggered
    • MAX — the Max daily measure triggered
    Dim badges (no fill) mean the measure didn't trigger — but the value is still shown so you can see how close it was.
  • Click a ticker to open its detail page.

How to read a stock dashboard

Each detail page shows the same data through the lens of one ticker. The layout, top to bottom:

  1. Header: ticker, company name, bucket (A or B), and the last close price as of the most recent trading session.
  2. Three variation cards: the exact numerical values used by the screen. Cards with amber borders and amber values triggered; the rest are shown for context. The formula appears below each card so you don't have to remember which is which.
  3. Candlestick chart: ~250 trading days of price history.
    • Each candle = one trading day. Green means the close was above the open; red means below.
    • The thin "wick" above and below each candle's body shows that day's full High–Low range.
    • Amber-tinted candles at the right edge are the last 5 sessions — the window the screen evaluates. This is the visual anchor for the variation numbers in the cards above.
    • Volume bars at the bottom of the chart in matching green/red.
    • The 1Y / 6M / 1M buttons re-slice the same in-memory data — no re-fetch, no flicker.
    • Bucket B charts default to a logarithmic y-axis so equal-percentage moves look like equal heights ($1 → $1.50 reads the same height as $10 → $15). Bucket A uses a linear axis.
  4. Stats block: last session date, 30-day average dollar volume, and a copy of the three trigger badges.
  5. Outbound links:
    • Yahoo Finance — quick price/news/financials.
    • SEC EDGAR — official filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K). We look up the CIK from SEC's ticker mapping so the link lands on the right company even for ADRs and similar.
    • News — Google News search for the ticker.

Filters applied before the variation math

Performance-wise, the variation math runs on a pre-filtered universe. Order of operations each day:

  1. Universe gate: common stock only. ETFs, ADRs from SPACs, warrants, preferred stock, and rights are dropped upstream in the universe-fetch step.
  2. Price band: Bucket A $300 ≤ last_close ≤ $800; Bucket B $0.79 ≤ last_close ≤ $20. Most of the ~6,500-name universe drops out here.
  3. Volume floor: 30-day average dollar volume ≥ $1,000,000. Filters illiquid names where a quoted "move" is meaningless.
  4. Variation math: only now do we compute the three measures and OR-combine for inclusion.

Data sources & subtleties

  • Screen data: Polygon.io Grouped Daily Bars — one API call returns OHLC for every US stock for a given trading day. Adjusted for splits and dividends.
  • Chart history: Yahoo Finance via yfinance. Used only for the ~500 tickers that make today's screen, which keeps request volume well under Yahoo's per-IP rate limit.
  • Split adjustment: both feeds use split-adjusted prices. A stock that did a 2-for-1 split last Tuesday won't show a fake −50% daily move — the historical leg of every comparison is already divided. This is correct behavior; the security genuinely didn't move in the split-adjusted frame.
  • Trading-session window: "trailing 5 sessions", not "5 calendar days." If holidays fall in the window, the math still uses 5 actual trading sessions. Computed via pandas_market_calendars's NYSE calendar.
  • "As of" timestamps: the screen reflects the most recent completed session. If today is Tuesday and we refresh at 7am ET, the screen uses Monday's close. The chart on detail pages may show fresher (intraday or pre-market) data because yfinance hits Yahoo's live feed; the screen uses Polygon's confirmed daily bar.

Known limitations

  • Descriptive, not predictive. A name on this list is volatile — nothing about whether that's an opportunity or a trap.
  • No intraday or after-hours data. The screen refreshes once per trading day, pre-market.
  • Common stock only — no ETFs, ADRs from SPACs, warrants, preferred, rights.
  • Universe drawn from NASDAQ Trader's symbol directory; small names not in that directory won't appear.
  • Polygon free tier has a 5-call/minute limit; rarely the final day's data can be missed on a given run (≤ 1 session of staleness).
  • This screen does not adjust for sector, market cap, or beta. A high-beta name and a normally-calm name with one shock day appear side-by-side.

How the screen works

· Range: max(High) − min(Low) ≥ $15
· 5-day return: |Close[t] − Close[t-5]| ≥ $15
· Max daily: max |daily ΔClose| ≥ $15
