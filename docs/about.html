How this works

Every US trading-day morning, this site refreshes a screen of NYSE + NASDAQ common stocks across two volatility buckets.

Buckets

  • Bucket A — Mid-priced movers: last close $300–$800 with ≥ $15 weekly variation by any of three definitions.
  • Bucket B — Penny movers: last close $0.79–$20 with ≥ 20% weekly variation by any of the same three definitions.

Both buckets require a 30-day average dollar volume ≥ $1,000,000 to filter out illiquid pump-and-dump candidates.

Three definitions of "weekly variation"

Computed over the trailing 5 trading sessions. A stock qualifies if any one is true.

Range = max(High) − min(Low)
5-day ret = |Close[t] − Close[t−5]|
Max daily = max |daily ΔClose|

Data

Screen data: Polygon.io Grouped Daily Bars. Chart history: Yahoo Finance via yfinance. Prices are split-adjusted; corporate actions handled by the upstream feeds.

Limitations

  • This is descriptive, not prescriptive. A ticker on the list is volatile — nothing about whether that's a buy or sell.
  • No intraday or after-hours data. Refresh is once per trading day, pre-market.
  • Common stock only (no ETFs, ADRs, warrants, preferred).
  • Universe drawn from NASDAQ Trader's symbol directory.

How the screen works
Trailing 5 trading days, OR-combined inclusion. Bucket A: $300–$800 with any of:
· Range: max(High) − min(Low) ≥ $15
· 5-day return: |Close[t] − Close[t-5]| ≥ $15
· Max daily: max |daily ΔClose| ≥ $15
Bucket B: $0.79–$20 with any of those expressed as 20% relative. Both buckets require
30-day avg dollar volume ≥ $1,000,000.
